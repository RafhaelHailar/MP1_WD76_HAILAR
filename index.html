Cabuyao

What to See

Churches

St. Polycarp Parish

St. Polycarp Parish, also known as "Cabuyao Church" of the Roman Catholic Diocese of San Pablo was established on 1571. It is located in Brgy. Poblacion, City of Cabuyao, Province of Laguna. The Parish Fiesta is celebrated every 23rd day of February.

St. Vicente Ferrer Shrine

Diocesan Shrine and Parish of San Vicente Ferrer of the Roman Catholic Diocese of San Pablo was established on 1946. It is located in Brgy. Mamatid, City of Cabuyao, Province of Laguna. The Parish Fiesta is celebrated every 5th day of April.

Famous Schools

Mapúa Malayan Colleges Laguna

Mapúa Malayan Colleges Laguna, formerly known as Malayan Colleges Laguna, is a private, research, and nonsectarian university wholly-owned subsidiary of the Mapúa University along Pulo–Diezmo Road in Barangay Diezmo, Cabuyao, Laguna. It was opened on June 18, 2006.

Pamantasan ng Cabuyao

The University of Cabuyao or Pamantasan ng Cabuyao is a university in the city of Cabuyao, province of Laguna, Philippines. It was founded by then Mayor Etok Aguillo, through the enactment of Municipal Ordinance 2003-059 approved on April 16, 2003.

City Hall

Cabuyao Town Plaza

Cabuyao City Plaza is a park, town square located in Cabuyao. Cabuyao City Plaza

Cabuyao City Hall

Cabuyao City Hall is a town hall in Cabuyao, Laguna located on F. B. Bailon Road. Cabuyao City Hall is situated nearby to the police station Cabuyao City Police and Cabuyao Fire Station.

Structures

Eternal Gardens Memorial Park

This haven of remembrance is designed to stand the test of time, providing a place for beautiful memories to be kept and treasured for generations to come,located at Memorial Park, Cabuyao, 4025 Laguna.

Cabuyao Twin Mansion

Cabuyao Mansion (formerly the Marcos Twin Mansion) is a government-owned mansion located at Brgy. Casile, Cabuyao. The mansion was built in the 1980s during the administration of the former Philippine president Ferdinand Marcos,located at Brgy. Casile, Cabuyao, Laguna.

Sta. Elena

Sta. Elena Fun Farm

The Fun Farm at Sta. Elena is an outdoor playground where both kids and grown-ups have access to animals and the natural world. It is here, where we offer a space to escape the fast-paced world of gadgets and the city. It is a place where kids are free to run wild in open spaces and connect with animals.

Sta. Elena Gold Club

Sta. Elena Golf Club has been included twice in the list of 100 Best Golf Courses Outside the United States by Golf Digest Magazine - the in 2005-2006 and the more recent one in 2009-2010. It is a prestigious award recognized by professionals in the industry.

Nature

Matang Tubig

Matang Tubig is a tourist spot located in the village (Balagbag Araw) and is the base of Barangay Casile in Cabuyao, Laguna. And it is part of the Canlubang Golf Course and Country Club and Marcos's House. The rivers flowed through the entire village of Calamba and Cabuyao. It was built in 1932 by the Spanish. And it is considered "Laguna Water Park". [1][2]

Cabuyao Fishing Port

Cabuyao Fishing Port is situated east of Phase 3 swimming pool, and southeast of the church Kapilya ni San Jose Asawa ni Maria.

Festivals

Batingaw Festival

A festivity commemorating the legendary Kampanang Ginto which Cabuyao is known. The celebration always starts with the simultaneous ringing of church bells, which Cabuyeños believe that it brings good agricultural harvest. Since the City of Cabuyao was founded by Miguel López de Legazpi on January 16, 1571, the City Government of Cabuyao celebrates "Cabuyao Day" every 16th day of January.

Santo Niño de Cabuyao Festival

The City of Cabuyao and its people celebrate the feast of the Child Jesus or Santo Niño in the Philippines every third (3rd) Sunday of January. It starts from a Mass at the Cabuyao Church followed by a festival in which all Cabuyeños call it as "Santo Niño de Cabuyao Festival". It is a parade of all Santos Niños along the City Proper. The festivities and tradition was started in 1981 by Alimagno family (then Judge Constancio Sr. and wife Mely) as a gesture of thanksgiving as their son Kennedy survived a near fatal accident in last quarter of 1979.
